Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM)

Main idea

The Capital Asset Pricing Model links expected return to systematic market risk. The central prediction is that investors are compensated only for risk that cannot be diversified away.

The CAPM equation

E[Ri] = Rf + βi(E[Rm] − Rf)
SymbolMeaning
E[Ri]Expected return on asset i
RfRisk-free interest rate
E[Rm]Expected market return
βiMarket beta of asset i

Beta

Beta measures how sensitive an asset is to movements in the market portfolio.

βi = Cov(Ri, Rm) Var(Rm)
  • β = 1: moves with the market.
  • β > 1: more volatile than the market.
  • β < 1: less volatile than the market.

Worked example

Suppose:

  • Risk-free rate: 3%
  • Expected market return: 9%
  • Beta: 1.5
E[Ri] = 3% + 1.5(9% − 3%) = 12%

The predicted expected return is therefore 12%.

Security Market Line

The Security Market Line relates beta to expected return. Its slope equals the market risk premium.

Slope = E[Rm] − Rf

Limitations

CAPM is elegant but relies on strong assumptions. Empirical finance has found additional return predictors beyond beta, including value, momentum, and size factors.
